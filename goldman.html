Goldman Sachs

A deep-dive into the college mental health care problem space.

Charlottesville needed skilled graduates to work on big problems. Yet cultural and financial barriers discouraged students from taking the non-traditional path.

Role:  Design, Behaviorial Research, Design Sprints, Rapid Prototyping
Team:  Daniel Autry (Solo)
Duration:  Feb 2018 (12 months)

Disclaimer: Due to non-discolosure agreements with Goldman Sachs, I am limited in the amount of work I can show.

Problem

Corporate bond traders require more seconds to execute high-speed trades.

In day-to-day client conversations, Goldman's bond traders have a mass of information they want to manage.

It's very time-consuming to sift through all the of the bond data, so the traders needed a way to reduce the repetitiveness of many of their tasks.

Solution

Present volatile bonds in a real-time web application, so that traders can find key nformation right away.

My task was to design a high-definition data visualizations to break down complex insights into simple and digestable UIs.

Being entirely responsible for the front-end of the app, I worked to gather feedback every step of the way.

Process

How do we allow traders to find bonds quickly?

    This required three phases:
  • Performing behaviorial interviews with each of the traders to understand behavior and pain points, as well as mapping out journey map.
  • Rapid prototype different solutions to present to traders.
  • Develop web-application by consolidating data points, taking feedback every step of the way.

User Research

Here are some of the main points of concern I found that traders faced.

Clients often demand corporate bond information immediately.

It's difficult to find corporate bonds quickly.

There's a lot of complex information to digest with corporate bonds.

There are often numerous bonds to search for at once.

Corporate bond data changes numerous times per day.

SEARCH

Most of the time, the trader knows the bond they are looking for beforehand. Therefore, search functionality made the most logical sense.

Using Twitter's Typeahead API, I implemented search functionality so that traders could look up bonds quickly.

Inspired by Google, I took a page out of their book to implement a design that was already familiar with traders.
